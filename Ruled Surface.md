Let $X$ be a surface (i.e. [[Scheme]] of dimension $2$) with a surjective morphism $\pi:X\rightarrow C$ to a nonsingular curve $C$ ([[Curve (Scheme)]]) s.t. the fivber $X_y$ is isomorphic to $\mathbb{P}^1$ ([[Projective Space]]) for each $y\in C$, and such that there exists a section of $\pi$, i.e. a morphism $\sigma : C\rightarrow X$ with $\pi\circ \sigma = id$

Then we have $p_a(X) = -g(C)$ and $p_g(X)=0$ ([[Arithmetic Genus]], [[Geometric Genus]])
